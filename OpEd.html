The future of the EU’s energy is nuclear

Alex Matulay, October 2022

The Ukrainian war is affecting every European citizen. Ukrainians are fighting for their survival and the rest of the continent is fighting the soaring energy prices that are caused by the greed of Vladimir Putin. The European Union is a net importer of energy. The European Commission estimated that 58% of the EU’s energy was imported in 2020. The world economic forum states that in 2021 the EU imported $108 billion worth of oil and natural gas from Russia. The Russian president has leverage on the EU’s energy security which can be observed since the invasion of Ukraine. The way forward is an energy-independent European Union which cannot be achieved without nuclear energy.

Nuclear power plants, which are expected to operate for at least 60 years since their construction, contribute to price stability and energy independence by providing a reliable source of energy. Nuclear energy is reliable because it doesn’t require constant refuelling. A typical nuclear power plant changes its source once every 18 to 24 months. Whereas coal or natural gas plants require a constant supply of fuel. Up until the invasion of Ukraine, natural gas has been mainly imported to the EU from Russia by pipelines. But since coal cannot flow through pipelines, the constant need to resupply coal power plants has exposed coal to a series of supply chain problems. For example, the drought season this summer has effectively closed the traffic in certain parts of the Rhine river in Germany which has affected the supply of coal to the EU’s power plants. Natural gas is unreliable because it is exposed to high price fluctuations. This can be observed in the market price development in the past two years. Tradingeconomics.com shows that the market price in August 2022 was 22 times higher than in April 2022. This has been caused mainly by the unreliability of the majority supplier: Russia. According to the world economic forum, Russian gas accounted for 40% of the European Union’s total gas consumption in 2021. Russia decreased the supply of natural gas to continental Europe by 60% since June 2021, breaching their contractual obligations, hence making themselves and natural gas no longer a reliable source of energy.

Nuclear energy has positive impacts on health and safety. It is the second safest and healthiest source of energy, after solar. Our world in data has estimated that death rates caused by air pollution and accidents from coal power plants cause 24.6 deaths per terawatt-hour whereas nuclear only 0.03. This is important because CREA estimated that the costs of pollution from fossil fuels in 2018 resulted in $2.9 trillion or 3.3% of the world GDP. An estimated 4.5 million people died due to exposure to fossil fuel air pollution in the same year. The IEA states that nuclear energy, the second lowest producer of low-carbon energy generation, has reduced the CO2 emission by 2 years’ worth of global-related emissions in the last 50 years. It emits 273 times fewer greenhouse gases than coal, 240 times fewer than oil, and 163 times fewer than natural gas. Fossil fuels are the most dangerous and dirtiest sources of energy. Nuclear being one of the safest, safer than wind energy, still faces fears from the public due to previous accidents in Chernobyl and Fukushima nuclear power plants. The nuclear powerhouses built today are designed to achieve optimum safety and have multiple safety systems to minimize the cause of a disaster. They include a series of physical barriers between the radioactive core and the outside environment, equipment that prevents human failures and errors from developing into problems, comprehensive monitoring equipment, and systems to prevent significant radioactive releases. For example, the nuclear power plants built in the U.S.A. are built to survive external attacks such as missile or commercial aircraft strikes. Such attacks would not cause a radiation leak.

If the European Union wants to achieve energy price stability it has to end its dependence on unreliable, unsafe, and dirty sources of energy. The price of natural gas has increased 22 times in less than two years making cheap Russian gas a thing of the past. Russia has proven that they are an unreliable source of energy, and the EU must adapt to this new reality. They have a clear alternative in front of their eyes. It is time for Europeans to forget about the Chernobyl and Fukushima disasters because nuclear energy offers a reliable, clean, and very safe source of energy for decades to come.
